Sonic Stitches

Telepresent storytelling through collaborative embroidery

Arduino Processing Conductive Thread Textile Sensors

Prompt

What is the potential of fiber interfaces in collaborative storytelling activated through sound, movement, and touch?

Context

Collaborative embroidery, weaving, and quilting have been used to tell stories for generations across many cultures. These craft practices are slow — requiring makers (often women) to sit for hours. That slowness provides a natural affordance for facilitating conversation and sharing stories.

We explored the notion of food as placemaking: What foods remind you of home? Food evokes strong sentiments of belonging, identity, and social ties. We asked how food narratives could explore sensorial evocations of home, and how the materiality of our work might amplify that artistic intent.

Artistic Approach

The physicality of embroidery offers specific affordances: the practice is slow, collaborative, and builds a mindful connection to the artifact. When touching hand-sewn embroidery, you can feel the thoughtful work that went into the piece — the journey of the craft maker.

By playing the stories of the craft maker while you directly interact with their stitchwork, artifact and voice become entangled.

Part 1
Gather Stories

Communities are invited to sew their own patterns into the tablecloth. As they sit and sew, they reflect on a meaningful question — e.g., "What food reminds you most of home?" Their responses are recorded and linked to their stitchwork.

Part 2
Share Stories

Guests sit for dinner on the Sonic Stitches tablecloth. Running their hands along hand-stitched patterns plays audio snippets from the person who sewed that portion — past and present voices at the same table.

Research & Materials

  • Conductive thread — carries electricity like wires; enables soft circuit construction using tufting and knitting techniques
  • Pressure sensor fabrics — measure physical pressure, weight, and squeezing; mapped to various sound outputs
  • Capacitive sensing — the circuit itself acts as a capacitive sensor, detecting finger proximity and touch

Scenarios

Scenario 1
Individual Exploration

A user traces their finger along the fabric to explore audio stories woven into a tapestry — a glimpse of dinnertime conversations from the past.

Scenario 2
Shared Storytelling

Collocated sharing where conversations of the past interleave with those physically present — a family reunion at the dinner table.

Scenario 3
Textile Mapping

A knitted object is directly associated with its audio story — computation disappears into everyday objects.

Technical Pipeline

Input
Conductive threads & capacitive sensors in fabric
→
Processing
Arduino IDE reads pressure; sends data to Processing IDE
→
Output
Speaker plays oral narratives triggered by touch

Future Directions

  • Advance from proof-of-concept to a full prototype built with a community
  • Find more efficient stitching methods with conductive materials for beginners
  • Improve hardware integration between the Arduino/breadboard and the tablecloth
  • Creatively record stories that "cross" narratives — playing on the collaborative, interwoven nature of the piece
